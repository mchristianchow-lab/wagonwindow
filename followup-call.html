Follow-Up Call
Instant Estimate Confirmation
1
Opening the Call
Confirm who you're speaking to
▼
Say this
"Hi, can I speak with [Customer Name]? … Hi [Name], it's [Your Name] calling from Wagon Windows — you submitted an instant estimate on our website for window cleaning. I'm just calling to go over a few quick details and get you confirmed. Is now an okay time?"
If they say it's a bad time — ask for a better time and call back. Don't rush through the questions.
✓
Got the right person on the line
✓
They have a couple of minutes
2
Confirm the Basics
Address, property type, contact
▼
Say this
"I've got your estimate in front of me. I just want to confirm a few things to make sure the price is accurate for your place."
✓
Confirm full service address
Ask even if you have it — people sometimes submit from a different device or address.
✓
Confirm city / area
Salmon Arm, Sorrento, Blind Bay, Sicamous, etc. — affects scheduling and drive time.
✓
Best phone number to reach them
Confirm it matches what they submitted. Get a backup if it's a landline.
✓
Property type matches estimate
House / townhouse / condo. Condo may have restrictions on exterior work.
3
Verify the Scope
Pane count, service type, add-ons
▼
Say this
"The estimate you got was based on [X] panes and [service type]. I just want to make sure that's accurate — sometimes people estimate a bit differently than how we count."
How we count: Each individual piece of glass = 1 pane. A standard window = 2 panes. A sliding patio door = 2 panes. A skylight = 1 pane. If their count is off, adjust the quote — don't just confirm a number that's wrong.
✓
Pane count sounds right for the home
Ask: "How many windows would you say you have roughly — is [X] about right?" If off by more than 5, recalculate.
✓
Exterior only or interior + exterior confirmed
If interior: someone must be home the whole time. Confirm this upfront.
✓
Any large / oversized windows
Picture windows, floor-to-ceiling, bay windows — $5 extra per pane. Worth asking if not already on the estimate.
✓
Screens included / confirmed count
Do all windows have screens? Any damaged or missing ones we should know about?
✓
Track cleaning confirmed
Some customers don't realize this is separate. Confirm they want it if it's on the quote.
✓
Gutter cleaning — if selected, confirm length
Ask if there are any areas that are hard to access (steep pitch, tall sections, attached garage).
✓
Hard water stains — if selected, which windows
We assess on arrival and confirm price before starting — let them know. Typically near sprinklers, hose areas, or bathroom windows.
4
Access & Property Notes
Everything that affects showing up
▼
Say this
"A few quick logistical things — just want to make sure we can get to everything when we arrive."
✓
Gate or fence — is there a code or do they need to be home to open it?
✓
Dog in the yard?
Ask if it needs to be secured or if there's a safe area to work. Important for exterior.
✓
Any windows that are hard to reach
Two-storey sections, steeply pitched roof areas, windows above a deck or retaining wall. We bring ladders but good to know in advance.
✓
Water source available outside
We need a working outdoor tap. If there isn't one, let us know — we may be able to bring water.
✓
Will someone be home when we arrive?
Required for all jobs — waiver and payment happen before work starts. For exterior-only they can leave after that, as long as we have backyard access.
✓
Any window condition issues to flag
Cracked seals (foggy glass), broken frames, stuck windows — good to know before we arrive. These don't affect cleaning but can set expectations.
✓
Any plants, furniture, or objects near windows
We work around them but good to know about rose bushes, deck furniture right against the house, etc.
5
Date, Time & Payment
Lock in the booking
▼
Say this
"Perfect — sounds like we're good to go. Let me get you booked in. What does your schedule look like? We're generally available [days you work] and we like to give a 2-hour arrival window so we're not locking you to an exact time."
✓
Preferred date confirmed
Offer 2–3 options if possible rather than asking open-ended "when works for you?"
✓
Morning or afternoon preference
✓
Payment method confirmed — cash or e-transfer
Payment is collected on arrival, at the same time as signing the waiver — before work begins. Let them know upfront so there are no surprises on the day.
✓
Someone will be home and available to pay at arrival
Payment and waiver happen before we start. If no one is home when we arrive, we can't begin the job.
Say this about payment:
"Just so you know how it works on our end — when we arrive we'll confirm the final price with you, have you sign a quick service waiver, and collect payment before we get started. It's cash or e-transfer. That way everything's agreed upfront and there are no surprises."
If they ask for a hard time (not a window):
"We try to give a 2-hour window because drive times between jobs can vary — but I'll text you the morning of with a more accurate ETA. Does that work?"
If they want to pay by credit card:
"We do cash or e-transfer right now — e-transfer's really quick, you can send it right from your phone when we arrive. Does that work for you?"
If they won't be home:
"We do need someone there when we arrive — payment and the waiver happen before we start work. Is there another day where you'd be around, or someone else who could be there?"
6
Confirm the Quote & Close
Set expectations, send confirmation
▼
Say this
"Just to confirm — your online estimate came to [price]. When we arrive we'll do a quick walk-around, lock in the final price, have you sign a short service waiver, and collect payment before we get started. Cash or e-transfer, whichever works for you.

I'll send you a confirmation text now with the date, time window, and our number. If anything comes up, just text us."
Send a confirmation text immediately after the call. Include: their name, address, date, time window, estimated price range, and your number. Customers who get a written confirmation are much less likely to cancel or forget.
