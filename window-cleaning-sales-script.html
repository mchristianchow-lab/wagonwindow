Door-to-Door Sales Script

Wagon Windows · Salmon Arm & the Shuswap · Field Edition

Standard → Premium → Supreme
Framework: Top-Down Anchoring with a live quote tool as your prop. Count panes with the customer, pull out the iPad, show them the three packages with real prices — no guessing, no credibility gap.
Price Anchoring Social Proof Live Quote Tool Assumptive Close
1
The Opener — Low-Pressure Local Context
Lower their guard. You're the local crew already on the block — not a cold caller.
If you just finished a nearby job:
"Hey, sorry to bother you — my name's [Name] with Wagon Windows. We just finished up at [neighbour's address / the corner house] and I noticed your windows are showing a bit of that seasonal buildup. We're already staged in the area so I wanted to see if it made sense to get you a quick quote while we're right here."
If you're canvassing cold:
"Hey, sorry to catch you — my name's [Name] with Wagon Windows. We're locally based out of Salmon Arm and we're doing a run through the neighbourhood today giving free quotes. Takes about two minutes. Do you mind if I ask when you last had your windows done professionally?"
Why: "Sorry to bother you" disarms immediately. Mentioning a neighbour's address activates social proof before you've said anything about price. Ending with a question gets them talking instead of you pitching.
2
The Walk-Around — Count the Panes Together
Physical movement builds commitment. Get them moving with you — they're already saying yes.
"Rather than throwing a number at you blind, I want to do it properly — takes 60 seconds. We just walk the outside, count the panes, and I can give you a live price right there on my phone. Does the left or right side of the house give better access to the back?"
📱
Open the Wagon Windows Field Tool on your phone or iPad as you walk.
→ Tap New Quote (or Resume Last Quote if you were mid-count)
→ Count as you walk — three types:
   Standard — normal double-hung, sliding, casement
   Large (+$8/pane) — floor-to-ceiling, picture windows, oversized fixed panes
   French / Complicated (+$8/pane) — divided lights, arched, leaded, multi-grid
→ By the time you're back at the front door, the total's in — ready to show packages
Why: "Left or right side" forces a micro-yes — they're choosing how to engage, not whether to. Counting panes together gives them ownership of the number. When you show the price, it's their data feeding it.
3
The Quote — Show the Packages Live
Anchor on Supreme. Make Premium the obvious smart choice. Standard is the fallback, not the goal.
"Alright — based on the [X] panes we counted, here's what the three packages look like for your place."
📱
Hand them the iPad / flip the phone toward them. Tap through the three packages so they see the live prices.
→ Supreme card shows at the top — they see the gold price first (anchor)
→ Premium is in the middle — red, "most common"
→ Standard is at the bottom — the safe fallback
→ Let them look for 5–10 seconds before you speak

"Most people we've done in this neighbourhood go with the Premium — you get both sides of every window cleaned inside and out. It's the one that makes the most sense if you're doing it once or twice a year.

The Supreme is the full service — same as Premium but we clean all the screens and tracks while we're already set up. Everything done in one visit. The Standard is there if you just want the exterior done quick and clean."
Why: Supreme anchors the ceiling — every price after it feels reasonable. Framing Premium as "most common in the neighbourhood" is social proof without being pushy. Standard stays available as a dignified fallback so no one leaves with nothing.
⚠
Travel Fee — Outside Salmon Arm Core
Flag it early. People respect honesty more than surprises.
If the job is in a travel zone
Nearby (Canoe, Tappen, Blind Bay, Sorrento, Silver Creek, etc.)+$35 travel fee — mention it when you name the price: "...that comes to [X], plus a $35 travel fee since we're coming out your way."
Further out (Chase, Falkland, Sicamous, etc.)+$55 travel fee — same approach: name it alongside the package price, not as an afterthought.
Why: Mentioning it in the same breath as the quote price frames it as a normal cost of the job, not a penalty. Burying it until booking creates objections and cancellations.
4
The Close — Ask for a Preference, Not Permission
Move from "Should I?" to "Which one?" — both answers are a yes.
"We're back in this area [day/week]. If I can get you slotted in now, I can lock in these prices.

Are you thinking the Premium — inside and out — or the Supreme where we knock out the screens and tracks at the same time?"
Why: The time anchor ("back in this area") creates soft urgency — real and credible. The closing question only offers Premium vs. Supreme. Standard isn't mentioned — if they bring it up, that's fine, but you don't volunteer it. Both options move forward.
If they hesitate
"Too much right now""No worries — want to start with just the exterior? Standard is [price] and it still makes a big difference. Minimum job is $119, so it's only worth it if there are enough panes to get there."
"Need to think""Of course — what's the main thing you're weighing up? Sometimes I can answer it right here."
"Talk to spouse""Absolutely — can I text you the quote so you've got it handy to show them?"
"Not today""That's fine. Can I grab your number and reach out when we're next in the area?"
5
Record the Sale — Lock It In on the Field Tool
Don't let a verbal yes turn into a no-show. Get the details in before you leave the door.
✅
Tap "They Said YES — Record Sale" on the quote screen.
→ Enter their name, phone, address, preferred date, and any notes (gate code, dog, etc.)
→ Tap Save + Book on Google Calendar to book them on the spot
→ Or Save Lead if you'll confirm date by text later
At minimum, get a phone number before you leave. A name + number with no date is still a hot lead. A verbal yes with nothing written down is worth nothing — people forget, plans change, and you lose the sale.
"Perfect. Let me grab your name and number so I can send you a confirmation text — and I'll put you down for [day]. You don't pay anything until the job's done and you're happy with it."
✓
Why This Works — Field Cheat Sheet
The psychology behind the pitch.
The Neighbour Hook
Social proof before you say a word about price. You're the crew already on the street — not a cold caller.
The Walk-Around
Count as you go — Standard, Large, French. Categorizing together gives them ownership of the number. When they see the price, it feels fair because their windows fed it.
Price Anchoring
Supreme sets the ceiling. Premium feels reasonable. Standard feels cheap by comparison — even though it's still your real minimum ticket.
Live Tool as Prop
Showing a live price on a phone signals transparency and professionalism. No scribbled numbers — a tool that calculates it in real time is a trust signal.
Choice Close
"Premium or Supreme?" eliminates the yes/no decision. Both options move forward. Standard isn't on the table until they bring it up.
Record Before You Leave
A phone number is the floor. A booked date is the goal. Every step you complete at the door is one less reason they have to cancel later.
